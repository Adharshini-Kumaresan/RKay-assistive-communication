# 🗣️ RKAY – Assistive Communication System

**RKAY** is an open-source assistive communication system designed to help individuals who cannot speak due to medical or neurological conditions (e.g., ALS, stroke, paralysis, vocal impairments).  
It restores the ability to express **intent, needs, and emotions** using **multimodal inputs** such as weak audio signals, lip movements, and contextual cues.

> Built for real-world usability, ethical AI research, and meaningful social impact.

---

## 🌍 Why RKAY?

Millions of people worldwide live with conditions that limit or completely remove their ability to speak. Existing solutions are often:
- Expensive  
- Non-customizable  
- Limited to single input modalities  

**RKAY aims to change that** by providing a flexible, open, and research-driven platform for assistive communication.

---

## ✨ Key Features (Planned & In Progress)

- 🎙️ **Weak Audio Signal Processing**  
  Enhance and interpret low-volume or impaired speech signals.

- 👄 **Lip Movement & Visual Cues Analysis**  
  Assist communication using lip-reading and facial motion cues.

- 🧠 **Context-Aware Intent Detection**  
  Infer user intent using environmental and interaction context.

- 🔀 **Multimodal Fusion**  
  Combine audio, visual, and contextual signals for robust communication.

- ♿ **Accessibility-First Design**  
  Designed with inclusivity, usability, and dignity at the core.
